About Us

Our Objectives

The Society of Jesus, a Christian Religious Order founded by St. Ignatius of Loyola in 1540, has been active in the field of education throughout the world since its origin. In India the Society of Jesus is at present responsible for over 350 High Schools, 45 University Colleges, 27 Technical Institutes and 8 Business Institutes with over 13000 lay teachers. They educate about 4, 50, 000 young people belonging to every class, community and linguistic group through the medium of English and other regional languages. These institutions are part of the Catholic Church's effort to share in the country's educational undertaking. This effort while being particularly directed towards the Christian community has always been at the service of the whole nation.
Jesuit Education is inspired by a vision of a man drawn from the life and teaching of Jesus Christ who has always been respected and admired by the people of India. It is also based on the principles of character formation elaborated by St. Ignatius of Loyola. This vision and these principles give the Jesuit institutions a specific character and set before their staff and students high ideals of humanism and service towards which they are invited to strive continually. Such an education inspires the students with:
1. A deep sense of religious and moral principles which help the students to be open to their fellow human beings and to God.
2. A sense of social responsibility and commitment.
3. A desire for enlightened leadership committed to national service and development.
4. An appreciation of Indian and world cultures.
5. A sense of creative responsibility, freedom and maturity. ST. XAVIER'S COLLEGIATE SCHOOL, Maharo is established by Dumka-Raiganj Province of the Society of Jesus. It is administered and run by the 'Jesuits of Santal Society', a minority Christian Society registered under the Society's Registration Act 1860, having its headquarters in Dumka. It is a private Christian minority school receiving no grant from the Government. The medium of instruction is English. It will seek affiliation with the Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations, New Delhi for conducting the Secondary School examinations.

Our Vision

Our vision for the school is to create an inclusive and innovative learning environment that empowers students to thrive academically, socially, and emotionally. We aspire to be a beacon of excellence in education, dedicated to nurturing the holistic development of each student. In our school, we envision a curriculum that goes beyond textbooks, fostering critical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving skills. We aim to inspire a love for learning by offering a diverse range of subjects and extracurricular activities, tailored to individual talents and interests. Our commitment to inclusivity means that every student, regardless of their background or abilities, will find a supportive community that embraces diversity and encourages mutual respect.
We envision a school culture that values empathy, tolerance, and global awareness. We strive to cultivate future leaders who are not only academically proficient but also compassionate and socially responsible.
Our vision includes partnerships with the community and collaboration with parents to ensure that each student reaches their full potential. In essence, our school's vision is to provide an enriching educational experience that prepares students to excel in a rapidly changing world while instilling values that make them responsible and empathetic global citizens.
ST. XAVIER'S COLLEGIATE SCHOOL aims at integral and total development of the human person through liberal and scientific knowledge, character formation and personality development. It strives to form men and women of competence, conscience and compassionate commitment, who are intellectually competent, morally upright, socially committed, spiritually inspired and dedicated 'men and women for others' in the service of the nation. Hence, the school commits itself to :
Achieve high standard of academic excellence by providing well planned courses and programmes, and maintain conducive academic atmosphere in the campus. Assist in the total formation of each individual through character formation, value education, leadership qualities and discipline.

1. Promote social justice, harmony and brotherhood in society
2. Uphold the dignity and equality of every human person irrespective of caste and creed
3. Promote respect for every religion and culture.
4. Preserve ecology and environment for healthy living.
5. Offer personal accompaniment through individual care and concern for one's growth and development.
6. Empower the less privileged and vulnerable sections of society, the tribals, dalits, women etc.
